Project Type

Coop Project, Service Canada, 2017.

Personal Role

Front-End Web Design.

Summary

A redesign of a very outdate wiki hub for new-hire students at Service Canada.

The Goal

This project was quite straightforward: I was asked to update an old webpage full of useful links and resources that the many Coop students hired by Service Canada will most likely need. The goal was to modernize the visual style and breakdown the information in a easy to follow layout.

The biggest challenge would come due to the fact that this was an internal 'intranet' page that was built upon a pretty outdate wiki-page system, very similar to the style of Wikipedia. This meant that the pages would load a lot of additional side content that in this case really wasn't needed and only served to constrain the visual style. Thus, I was determined to figure out a way to impose a modern and clean looking series of webpages on top of the standart wiki UI style.

The Final Design

Shortly after receiving the project, I met with a colleague who worked on the web content team to seek some guidance on the topic. They reffered me to a recently created internal page created to advertise an upcoming event. This was the first example I was able to find of someone breaking out of the default wiki style and thankfully, I was able to study they pages to understand their solution. Through this I was able to arrive at a blank page, escaping the outdated wiki template.

Before begining the visual design, I met with a few other Coop students to learn more about which of the resources were most often needed and which weren't and use this information to guide the layout of my page/pages. Once I was settled on the sitemap and layout, it was simple HTML + CSS design to create it.
